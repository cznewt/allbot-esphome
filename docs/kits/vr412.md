# VR412 — 4-legged ALLBOT with ankles

No dedicated Whadda manual; treat this as a VR408 ([manual id=383](https://manuals.whadda.com/article.php?id=383)) extended with per-leg ankle servos and ankle parts from the [ALLBOT Add-ons](https://manuals.whadda.com/category.php?id=208) category. The configuration corresponds to the `examples/VR412` sketch in Velleman's ALLBOT-lib.

## Kit at a glance

- 12 × 9 g servos: 4 hips + 4 knees + 4 ankles (one extra joint per leg vs. VR408)
- VR408 mechanical backbone and legs, plus four ankle servo brackets
- Original electronics: Arduino UNO + VRSSM servo shield + VRBS1 battery shield

The extra ankle lets the foot tilt independently from the knee — that's what makes `standup`, `lean_*`, and the wave gaits look more natural than on VR408.

## Mechanical build

Assemble the VR408 chassis per [article 383](https://manuals.whadda.com/article.php?id=383), then add the ankle servo + foot extension on each leg. All the "centre the servo at its initial angle before clipping the horn on" guidance still applies — but the initial angles differ by joint type:

| Joint | Initial angle |
| --- | --- |
| Hips | 45° (legs splayed forward-out) |
| Knees | 10° (legs folded under the body at rest) |
| Ankles | 0° (feet tucked flat against the leg) |

The `standup` action gently rotates the knees to 110° and then each ankle to 20° in sequence to lift the body off the ground — this is the first action you should call after boot.

`flipped` flags mirror the original `VR412.ino` `BOT.attach(...)` arguments one-for-one.

## Gaits exposed as Home Assistant actions

`standup`, `walk_forward`, `walk_backward`, `walk_left`, `walk_right`, `turn_left`, `turn_right`, `lean_{forward,backward,left,right}`, `look_{left,right}`, `wave_{front,rear}_{left,right}`, `scared`, `chirp`, `stop`.

The walk gaits are step-lift-drop sequences — knee lifts the leg, hip swings it forward, knee drops, hip pushes the body — and the ankle barely moves. The ankle comes into play for `lean_*` (ankle tilts + knee bends) and `wave_*` (knee lifts the leg high, ankle wags).

## First-run sequence

1. Flash `example/vr412.yaml`.
2. Prop the robot on a book — feet off the ground.
3. Call `standup` from HA. Watch each leg lift and settle; if any knee/ankle pair looks wrong (leg collapses, foot points the wrong way), fix that servo's calibration before going further.
4. Call `chirp`, then `look_left` / `look_right` (hips only — cheapest sanity check).
5. Set on the floor, call `walk_forward`.

## Why it trips when VR408 didn't

Extra ankles mean more degrees of freedom and more things that can be miscalibrated. If a walk gait tips the robot forward, it's nearly always an ankle offset — the foot isn't flat during the "push" phase so the whole leg rolls. Adjust the relevant `ankle_*` with `offset:` in YAML; a ±5° tweak usually fixes it.

# VR612 — Hexapod ALLBOT

No dedicated Whadda manual; this is an extended ALLBOT configuration supported by the `examples/VR612` sketch in Velleman's ALLBOT-lib. Parent category: [Robotics & ALLBOTs](https://manuals.whadda.com/category.php?id=85) · [ALLBOT Add-ons](https://manuals.whadda.com/category.php?id=208).

## Kit at a glance

- 12 × 9 g servos: 6 hips + 6 knees (hip + knee per leg, three legs per side)
- Extended backbone with middle-leg brackets
- Original electronics: Arduino UNO + VRSSM + VRBS1

## Mechanical notes

The hexapod geometry matters: middle legs are perpendicular to the body, front and rear legs splay out at ±45°. That's why hip initial angles differ between front/rear (45°) and middle (90°) — the middle leg hips don't need to rotate the leg around the body.

| Joint | Initial angle |
| --- | --- |
| Front/rear hips | 45° |
| Middle hips | 90° |
| All knees | 20° (legs tucked under the body at rest) |

## Tripod gait

All six-legged walking in the VR612 port uses the classic **tripod gait**: legs are split into two sets of three that move in alternation:

- **Group 3a:** front-left + rear-left + middle-right
- **Group 3b:** front-right + rear-right + middle-left

At any moment one group is lifted and swinging while the other is planted and pushing the body. This is why `walk_forward` toggles between those three-at-a-time lift + push moves — it's not three pairs of moving legs, it's two alternating tripods.

## Gaits exposed as Home Assistant actions

`walk_forward`, `walk_backward`, `walk_left`, `walk_right`, `turn_left`, `turn_right`, `lean_{forward,backward,left,right}`, `look_{left,right}`, `wave_{front,rear}_{left,right}`, `scared`, `chirp`, `stop`.

`walk_left` / `walk_right` are interesting on a hexapod — they work by shifting hips laterally instead of pushing forward/back, using the middle legs as the pivot.

## First-run sequence

1. Flash `example/vr612.yaml`.
2. Prop the robot up so legs dangle free.
3. Call `chirp`, then `look_left` / `look_right` (hips only).
4. Call `lean_forward` — the front knees bend, robot tips forward; safe because all six legs stay on the ground.
5. Set on the floor, call `walk_forward`.

## Tuning the tripod

If the robot drifts sideways while walking forward, one tripod group is stepping further than the other. Check each knee's `offset:` — a single degree of asymmetry compounds over many steps. If it trips forward, it's a hip push-through angle; dial down the "swivel forward" (`hip_* → 80` / `5`) moves by a few degrees in YAML.

# VR618 — Hexapod ALLBOT with ankles

No dedicated Whadda manual; this is the most ambitious ALLBOT configuration, supported by the `examples/VR618` sketch in Velleman's ALLBOT-lib. Parent category: [Robotics & ALLBOTs](https://manuals.whadda.com/category.php?id=85) · [ALLBOT Add-ons](https://manuals.whadda.com/category.php?id=208).

VR618 = VR612 geometry (6 legs) + VR412-style per-leg ankle = 3 degrees of freedom per leg × 6 legs = **18 servos**.

## Kit at a glance

- 18 × 9 g servos: 6 hips + 6 knees + 6 ankles
- Extended hexapod backbone with middle-leg brackets and ankle joints
- Original electronics: Arduino Mega (UNO runs out of PWM channels) + servo control shield + battery

Because of the servo count, this is the one kit where you need **two PCA9685 boards** instead of one.

## PCA9685 wiring

Solder the A0 address jumper on the second board so it responds at `0x41`. Both boards share the same I²C bus, 3.3 V logic supply, and 5–6 V servo rail. `example/vr618.yaml` puts all hips and knees on board A (`0x40`, 12 channels) and all ankles on board B (`0x41`, first 6 channels) — that grouping keeps the wiring tidy.

```
ESP32          PCA9685 A (0x40)        PCA9685 B (0x41)
GPIO21 ───┬──── SDA             ┬──── SDA
GPIO22 ───┼──── SCL             ┼──── SCL
3V3    ───┼──── VCC             ┼──── VCC
GND    ───┼──── GND             ┼──── GND
6 V rail ─┴──── V+              ┴──── V+
```

At 18 servos the current budget matters — size the servo supply for 3 A+ peak, more if you go to larger MG90S-class servos.

## Tripod gait (same as VR612, plus ankles)

Legs still split into two alternating tripods:

- **Group 3a:** front-right + rear-right + middle-left
- **Group 3b:** front-left + rear-left + middle-right

The ankles do what they do on VR412 — they tilt during the "drop" phase of each stride so the foot contacts the ground flat instead of edge-first, giving noticeably more grip on smooth floors. Watch the `ankle_* → 20` moves in `walk_forward`: those are the ankle tilt-ins during foot placement.

## Gaits exposed as Home Assistant actions

`standup`, `walk_forward`, `walk_backward`, `turn_left`, `turn_right`, `lean_{forward,backward,left,right}`, `wave_{front,rear}_{left,right}`, `chirp`, `stop`.

No `walk_left` / `walk_right` or `look_*` in the original VR618 sketch — they aren't defined upstream, so they aren't in the YAML. If you want them, copy the VR612 versions and add the ankle nudges.

## First-run sequence

1. Flash `example/vr618.yaml`. Confirm both PCA9685s show up in the ESPHome log.
2. Prop the robot on a book. All 18 legs should dangle free.
3. Call `standup` — knees rotate from 135° to 100° and lift the body. This is your first sanity check on servo calibration.
4. If anything looks wrong, fix it now before any walking gait is attempted.
5. Call `lean_forward` and `lean_backward` — these exercise ankles + knees together without needing dynamic balance.
6. Set on the floor, call `walk_forward`.

## Where it tends to fail

- **PCA9685 #2 not detected:** address jumper not soldered, or SDA/SCL not bridged to the second board.
- **Some legs won't lift:** 18 servos × stall current exceeds your power supply. Measure the rail voltage during `standup` — if it dips below 4.5 V, upgrade the supply.
- **Asymmetric tripod drift:** identical to VR612 — one tripod steps further than the other. The ankle offsets compound the symptom; audit both knee and ankle `offset:` values on the drifting tripod.
